# Source
IRLSSG 2014 diagnostic criteria

# Intended Use
Used for educational RLS screening logic only. Not for direct diagnosis.

# Summary
- The core diagnostic framework describes five essential points: an urge to move the legs that is usually accompanied by uncomfortable sensations; symptom onset or worsening during rest or inactivity; partial or complete relief with movement; stronger expression in the evening or at night; and symptoms that are not solely accounted for by another medical or behavioral condition.
- For education inside this app, the practical implication is that users can be taught to track whether symptoms are linked to rest, whether movement helps, whether the pattern is evening-predominant, and whether common mimics such as cramps, positional discomfort, neuropathy, venous disease, arthritis, or medication-related restlessness may also fit.
- The output of this app must stay at the level of educational screening and symptom logging. It must not conclude that a user definitively has RLS.
