# Health education prescription format and boundaries

Source: DB34/T 4701-2024 health education prescription specification; diabetes/nutrition education prescription referral form; patient education materials list for metabolic conditions.

Intended Use: Backend formatting and safety-boundary reference for education-only sleep-health prescriptions. Do not treat as a medical prescription, diagnosis, medication order, device order, or individualized treatment plan.

Health education prescriptions should be structured, readable, and action-oriented. A patient-facing prescription should include a clear title, the target user or patient context, the health problem, a brief condition summary, key symptoms to track, major risk factors or contributors to review, categorized health guidance, other individualized guidance, use instructions, and a contact or referral boundary.

For this sleep-health app, the prescription must remain educational. It may support self-care, symptom tracking, lifestyle guidance, and clinician-preparation notes. It must not diagnose RLS, insomnia, sleep apnea, or another disorder; it must not prescribe medication, suggest medication doses, recommend iron treatment, set CPAP or other device parameters, or recommend buying treatment devices. When medication, device, pregnancy, childhood symptoms, kidney disease, anemia, neurologic symptoms, breathing pauses, chest pain, drowsy driving, or major daytime impairment appear, the output should shift toward clinician or sleep-specialist review.
