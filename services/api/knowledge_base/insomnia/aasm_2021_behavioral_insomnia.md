# Source
AASM 2021 behavioral insomnia guideline

# Intended Use
Used to support high-level mention of behavioral treatment directions such as CBT-I.

# Summary
- The behavioral-treatment guideline supports nonpharmacologic approaches, especially structured behavioral care such as CBT-I, for chronic insomnia symptoms.
- In an educational agent, the safe user-facing message is that persistent insomnia often benefits from structured behavioral treatment and clinician assessment, rather than ad hoc self-medication.
- The app may mention CBT-I as an evidence-based direction, but it should not claim that a user has chronic insomnia or independently prescribe a treatment course.
