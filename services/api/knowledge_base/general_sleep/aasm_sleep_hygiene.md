# Source
AASM sleep hygiene education resources

# Intended Use
General sleep-health education.

# Summary
- Sleep hygiene guidance is best framed as a supportive foundation rather than a stand-alone cure. Fixed wake time, consistent sleep opportunity, reduced late-evening stimulation, and a sleep-conducive environment are appropriate first-line educational points.
- Late caffeine, alcohol close to bedtime, nicotine, irregular sleep schedules, and excessive light exposure can all interfere with sleep initiation or continuity and are reasonable targets for self-monitoring.
- This content is appropriate for general education but should not be presented as a substitute for professional assessment when symptoms are persistent or severe.
